Adayar 2 Store Visit Report

Store visited by Bharath

Visit Date: 28th October 2025

Report Summary

Visit Overview: The store visit was conducted to get the glimpse of the brand and the current VM practices.

Key Observations: The store has a big and elevated facade which is good for visibility but the entrance is small which doesn't give a grandeur feeling.

Overall Assessment: The visit revealed both strengths and areas requiring attention. Immediate action on the identified improvement areas will significantly enhance store performance and customer satisfaction.

Detailed Analysis

1
Facade
Observation: The facade looks good, welcoming, good parking space, the flagpole signage is also good.

Recommendation: The lighting of the store which looks to be at 3500 kelvin is premiumness but if it can be increased to 5500 kelvin it would make the store look more inviting from the outside.
2
Entrance
Observation: The Entrance is small, cramped and doesn't give a grand feeling.

Recommendation: Pay 0 for Lens on the glass is good but it should be somewhere inside, this has chance of missing out and the visual looks really old which hasn't been changed for a very long time.
3
Cash Counter
Observation: Cash counter looks empty .

Recommendation: there can be brand logo at the cashcounter to give a feeling of completeness
4
Campaign Glass Vinyl Backside
Observation: a Major real estate is being wasted at the back of the campaign glass vinyl which can be used for branding.

Recommendation: same campaign visual at the back without the clear vinyl part can be used to increase campaign awareness.
5
Focal to break the seriousness
Observation: The display is looking serious and formal which is good for the brand but a focal point to break the seriousness is missing.

Recommendation: Key visuals at the focal area are missing which can break the story telling for the store also the top area can be used for brand legacy.
6
Other feedbacks
Notes on Powered Sunglasses section: Covered in detail on VM implementation for this section.
